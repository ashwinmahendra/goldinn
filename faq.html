Your Guide to Fractional Ownership

Unlock Capital, Retain Control

1. What is fractional ownership?

Fractional ownership allows you to sell shares of your property to multiple investors. You can sell the entire property or only a portion of it while retaining operational control. Think of it like selling to a group of silent partners who share costs and benefits proportionally.

2. How does it work?

Legal Structure: The portion of the property offered for sale is held in separate entity, with investors owning shares.

Capital Raise: Sell all or a certain percentage of shares to fund goals like renovations, debt repayment, or expansion. eg. 30% or 50%

Investor Management: The platform handles all investor reporting, communciation, payouts, etc.

5. Will investors interfere with my operations?

No. Investors are passive shareholders with no voting rights. You (if you currently operate) or the property management company will run the property as usual.

6. What are tax implications?

Deductible Expenses: Renovations, management fees, and depreciation.

Tax Savings: Cost segregation can accelerate depreciation, reducing taxable income.

7. What if I want to sell later?

Buyback Option: Repurchase shares at market price.

Full Sale: The platform markets the property, with proceeds split by ownership.

8. How do you determine eligibility?

We prefer properties with strong occupancy, financials, reputation and value add potential (renovations, tech upgrades, etc.)

9. How are usage rights managed?

Automated Scheduling: Fair allocation via a digital calendar
